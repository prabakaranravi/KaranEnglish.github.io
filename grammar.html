Nouns

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

Examples:
  • Person – teacher, doctor
  • Place – school, city
  • Thing – book, phone
  • Idea – happiness, freedom
Example sentences:

The teacher is in the classroom.

I bought a new phone.

Pronouns

A pronoun replaces a noun.

Examples:
  • I
  • You
  • He
  • She
  • It
  • We
  • They
Example sentences:

John is my friend. He is very kind.

Sarah is my sister. She studies English.

Verbs

A verb shows an action.

Examples:
  • run
  • eat
  • study
  • write
Example sentences:

She studies English every day.

They play football after school.

Adjectives

An adjective describes a noun.

Examples:
  • big
  • happy
  • beautiful
Example sentences:

She has a beautiful dress.

The big building is a hotel.

Adverbs

An adverb describes a verb.

Examples:
  • quickly
  • slowly
  • carefully
Example sentences:

He runs quickly.

The teacher explains the lesson clearly.

Prepositions

Prepositions show position or relationship.

Examples:
  • in
  • on
  • at
  • under
  • between
Example sentences:

The book is on the table.

The cat is under the chair.

Conjunctions

Conjunctions connect words or sentences.

Examples:
  • and
  • but
  • or
  • because
Example sentences:

I like tea and coffee.

She was tired but she continued working.

Interjections

Interjections express emotion.

Examples:
  • Wow!
  • Oh!
  • Oops!
  • Hurray!
Example sentences:

Wow! That is amazing.

Oops! I made a mistake.

Tenses

Present Tense

I eat breakfast every morning.

She studies English.

Past Tense

I ate breakfast yesterday.

She studied English last night.

Future Tense

I will eat breakfast tomorrow.

She will study English tomorrow.

Active Voice and Passive Voice

Active:

The teacher explains the lesson.

Passive:

The lesson is explained by the teacher.

Direct and Indirect Speech

Direct:

She said, "I am happy."

Indirect:

She said that she was happy.

Modal Verbs

Examples:
  • can
  • could
  • may
  • might
  • must
  • should
Example sentences:

I can speak English.

You should practice daily.

Conditionals

Example sentences:

If you study, you will pass the exam.

If it rains, we will stay home.

Question Forms

Examples:

What is your name?

Where do you live?

Why are you learning English?
